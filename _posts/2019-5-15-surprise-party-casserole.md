### Ingredients

- Ground Beef (27% fat): 2.5 lbs
- Rice, cooked (I used Jasmine): 9 cups
- Zhenjiang Vinegar (black rice vinegar): 3.5 tablespoons
- Olive oil (Avocado or coconut oil would also work)
- Salt, preferably Himalayan
- Pepper, preferably fresh ground
- Vidalia or sweet onions: 2-3 medium
- Shredded cheese (I mostly used a blend of cheddar): 6 ounces


### Instructions

Brown the beef on low-medium heat with enough olive oil to coat the pan (I also added a bit more later), vinegar, salt, and pepper. I used a cast iron skillet and a copper pan but any pan will do.

[Caramelize][1] the onions (see link). This can take close to an hour.

Layer the rice, beef, onions, and cheese in a 9x13 baking pan. You can also mix the last three (or all four if you're feeling wild) together before layering.

Bake at 350&#8457; until the center is warm and the cheese is melted. The rice and beef were warm when I made mine so it only took about 20 minutes. If all of your ingredients are cold, I would suggest leaving the cheese off until the rest is getting warm and then add it.

Enjoy!


### Parting ~~Shots~~ Thoughts

This pares nicely with roasted cauliflower and/or broccoli. Coat the veggies in a mixture of olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, and cumin. Bake at 350&#8457; for 15-18 minutes or until you can easily cut the veggies with a fork. You could also make the veggies before-hand and mix them in with the meat, onions, and cheese.

Shredded chicken would *probably* substitute well for ground beef.

Next time I make this, I'm going to throw some more spices in. Probably some combination of the following: garlic, ginger, paprika, cumin, turmeric, and/or basil. A cumin-basil-chicken combo sounds particularly delicious.
