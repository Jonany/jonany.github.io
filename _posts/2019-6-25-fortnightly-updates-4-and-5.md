With vacation, a wedding, and general craziness, writing an update has been on the back burner. I would say I *didn't have time* but that's a mentality I try to avoid. It's better to acknowledge my priorities and change them if need be. Besides the traveling and events, there's been quite a lot going on with me over the past four weeks.

### Four weeks ago
I started on my first code contribution to an open-source project! :tada: [Mattermost][1] *is a flexible, open source messaging platform that meets even the most demanding privacy and security standards*. It's similar to Slack but has some key features that Slack is missing, threaded conversations being a big one for me. The project came to my attention through one of its job postings. The contribution guidelines were especially clear and setting up the dev environment took some tinkering but was pretty straightforward. Perhaps most important was that I found outstanding issues that I felt I could complete without having to learn a new language or dive deep into a code base. The issues I picked out involved adding new UI tests for the Mattermost web client. Mattermost uses [Cypress][3] for its UI tests which I'd read about but never used. It wasn't difficult to pick up since I'm familiar with UI and unit testing and the [Mocha][4]/[Chai][5] assertions Cypress uses have a similar style to [Jasmine][2], which I use at work.

### Three weeks ago
Went on vacation to Savannah, GA! If you're ever there, my top two recommendations are [The Andrew Low House][6] and the [Savannah National Wildlife Refuge][7], particularly the wildlife drive. Savannah is a great place to just walk around and enjoy the sights. The historic section is quite extensive and there are squares throughout the city. There is also a free transit system called [DOT][15] that runs a loop around the historic district.

### Two weeks ago
While trying to find a PC game I could pick up and play when I needed a break from job hunting and I found my copy of [Freelancer][8]!! :grinning: I used to play that game a lot before college but it's been years since I've played. The beginning was a little boring since I known the story and how to play the game (and how to make :moneybag: :grinning: *hint: it isn't by going on missions*) but after a few story points I got more into it. The [modding community][9] is still going strong despite the game being over 16 years old and there are a lot of great mods. I'm currently playing vanilla with a few unofficial patches but after I play through I'll probably try out some of the total conversion mods. [Discovery][10] was one I particularly enjoyed playing back in the day. If I ever get the time, I'd like to create a low-poly mod for the game. Low-poly is a style that I have soft spot for and I think it would look really good in the game.

### This past week
Made my first open source pull request!! :tada: It was for one of the Mattermost GitHub issues I started work on before vacation. The maintainers requested a few changes which I made and committed Saturday afternoon! Once I finish with the other test I'm working on, I'd like to take a stab at one of the their [GitHub issues][11] for the server. It's built with [Go][12] which is a language I'd like to learn. From what I've seen and read about it, it's similar to C which I've had experience with.
Besides working on the Mattermost tests, I've been learning more about [serverless architectures][13] and [emotional intelligence][14] on LinkedIn Learning. The serverless architecture course is helping me get a good grasp of what serverless is and how and when it can be used. The emotional intelligence course is about being aware of how other people are feeling or might feel in different situations.

### This coming week
Besides job hunting, I'm planning on finishing a LinkedIn Learning course on [design patterns][16] and (hopefully) submitting a pull request for the other Mattermost test. After the design patterns course, I'm deciding between courses on [Docker][17] and [Go][18]. There are also a few career, resume, and interviewing courses I have saved so we'll see what I actually end up watching. Maybe all of them! :laughing:
